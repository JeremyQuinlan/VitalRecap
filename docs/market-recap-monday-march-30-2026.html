MARKETS

The SPX dropped -0.39% to 6343 while the Nasdaq sank -0.73%. The Dow managed a small gain of +0.11%. The R2K tumbled -1.46%. Treasuries rallied with yields falling 7-9 basis points across the curve. RATES & FED

Treasury yields fell 7-9 basis points across the curve as investors sought safety. Fed expectations have shifted from 10 basis points of hikes to 5 basis points of cuts for the remainder of the year. Powell's Harvard appearance reiterated his March 18th remarks, maintaining that inflation ex-tariffs is nearly at target while oil shocks should be one-time events.

MARKET OUTLOOK

We're turning slightly more bullish given favorable technical conditions in positioning and sentiment, plus potential for Middle East news flow to improve at the margin. While escalation fallout would be significantly negative (as Iran intensifies economic terror campaigns), this prospect should keep Trump searching for offramps. The one wrinkle remains tech, which will likely continue struggling for the foreseeable future. Iran is gradually reopening Hormuz to certain traffic while Houthis haven't fired on ships recently.
