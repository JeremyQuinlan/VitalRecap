Mid-Day Update · Tuesday May 12, 2026

May 12, 2026 · 11:12 AM ET

MARKETS

SPX fell -0.72% while the equal-weight version dropped -0.74%. The Nasdaq slumped -1.22%, Dow declined -0.49%, and R2K tumbled -1.96%. Brent crude surged +3.65% to ~$108 amid geopolitical tensions. Gold fell -1.30%, silver sank -1.90%, and Bitcoin dropped ~-1.50% to ~$80.57K. DXY spiked +0.40% (USD up ~0.65% vs GBP).

RATES & FED

Treasury yields rose ~3-4 basis points across the curve, pressured by climbing oil prices, hot CPI data, and UK political turmoil pushing up gilt yields. The market is pricing in 10 basis points worth of Fed hikes this year.

MARKET OUTLOOK

This looks like normal and healthy profit-taking rather than panicked selling, particularly as chips finally take a breather after extreme parabolic moves. Iran-linked concerns are ratcheting higher following Trump's escalating rhetoric (calling the situation "totally unacceptable" and putting Iran on "life support") plus CNN reports that Trump is "more seriously considering a resumption of major combat operations." The hot CPI isn't helping sentiment, but the selling pressure remains orderly for now. Wednesday brings more catalysts with Chinese internet earnings, PPI data, and Trump's first Xi meeting scheduled for 10pm ET.

GEOPOLITICAL

Trump is "increasingly frustrated" with Iran negotiations and "more seriously considering a resumption of major combat operations than he has in recent weeks" (CNN). A UAE natural gas plant damaged by Iranian strike last month won't be fully operational until 2027, though it could reach 80% capacity by end-2026 (FT). Supply chain disruptions are causing plastics prices to surge, creating significant margin pressure across industries (Barron's).

COMPANY NEWS

C3.ai (AI) announced modestly better sales with meaningfully improved operating loss versus guidance, though bookings fell short. Thomas Siebel will return as CEO.

Aramark (ARMK) beat Q2 EPS expectations (49¢ vs 47¢ Street) driven by healthy revenue with organic growth >200 basis points ahead of estimates at ~12%. Now sees organic revenue growth toward high-end of prior +7-9% range.

JD.com (JD) delivered solid Q1 upside with EPS of RMB5.12 vs Street RMD3.56 and sales +4.9% to RMB315.7B. Core JD Retail business saw robust operating margin expansion (+70 basis points Y/Y to 5.6%).

Under Armour (UAA) provided soft F27 EPS guidance at ~10¢ mid-point, more than 50% below Street's 23¢ forecast, due to expected slight revenue decline and external cost headwinds.

Venture Global (VG) reported strong Q1 EBITDA upside ($1.37B vs $1.27B Street) and significantly raised full-year EBITDA guidance to $8.2-8.5B from prior $5.2-5.8B, benefiting from surging natural gas prices amid Iran conflict.

Zebra (ZBRA) beat Q1 EPS and EBITDA ($4.75/$347MM vs Street 4.25/$320MM) with margin expansion and raised full-year guidance (sales now +10-14% vs prior +9-13%, EPS $18.50 vs prior $18).
