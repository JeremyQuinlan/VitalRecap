MARKETS

The SPX dipped -0.12% while the Nasdaq rose +0.35%. Broader weakness showed elsewhere with equal-weight S&P down -0.71%, Dow -269 points/-0.56%, and R2K declining -0.22%. Brent crude fell ~150 basis points to ~$94.50. Gold dropped -0.15% while silver advanced +1.30% and bitcoin climbed +0.95%. The DXY dropped -0.15%.

RATES & FED

Treasuries faced selling pressure with yields rising 3-4 basis points across the curve, despite the significant drop in oil prices. Fed expectations held fairly steady throughout the session.

MARKET OUTLOOK

There's still abundant skepticism about the US-Iran ceasefire, so any durable détente should pressure the SPX higher. However, the index has already bounced meaningfully (about 8.4% trough-to-peak in just a few sessions) and is flirting with the 6900-7000 level that acted as difficult resistance even before the war started. It's very difficult for anyone to make the case that the war made the near and medium-term economic landscape better than it was before. The aggressive tech rotation continues as investors violently vomit out of software and pile into data center infrastructure plays.

GEOPOLITICAL

US-Iran negotiations in Pakistan remain the weekend's critical event, though an Iranian official suggested the meeting might not happen unless Lebanon ceasefire is declared and frozen assets released (multiple sources). Trump warned the Pentagon was "loading up the ships" with fresh ammunition to relaunch war should détente collapse, later telling NY Post that "The Iranians don't seem to realize they have no cards" (NY Post). China is playing a key role in Middle East peace negotiations, with Xi hoping this sets stage for successful Trump summit next month (WSJ). White House is deliberately adopting more conciliatory approach to China compared to Trump's first term antagonism (WSJ). Trump told NBC he was "very optimistic" about reaching a deal with Iran, noting Iranian leaders are "much more reasonable" in meetings than to press (NBC).

COMPANY NEWS

CRWV announced a multi-year agreement with Anthropic, joining the AI company's growing ecosystem of infrastructure partners.
