Vital Market Recap for Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 04:04 PM ET

MARKETS

The SPX surged +3.54% while the Nasdaq ripped +4.92%. The Dow gained 1125 points (+2.49%) and the R2K jumped +3.41%. DXY slumped 60 basis points, Brent fell 270 basis points to ~$104.50, while gold climbed +3.85% and silver advanced +7.35%. Bitcoin ramped +1.75% to ~$67.75K.

RATES & FED

Treasuries saw solid gains with yields dropping 3-7 basis points across the curve as geopolitical tensions showed signs of easing. The market is pricing in roughly 7 basis points of Fed Funds cuts this year, reflecting the ongoing policy uncertainty amid Middle East developments.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The Iran deescalation narrative is driving meaningful relief rallies, particularly as Trump confirmed WSJ reports about potentially exiting the conflict without militarily opening Hormuz (which dramatically reduces ground troop deployment odds). While bears rightfully point out that even if fighting stops tomorrow, energy will continue embedding significant geopolitical risk premiums and stagflationary impulses will persist for months. Still, the tea leaves increasingly point toward the White House moving further toward deescalation, with various officials suggesting strategic objectives have been largely achieved.

GEOPOLITICAL

Trump effectively confirmed WSJ reporting about potentially exiting the Iran conflict without militarily reopening Hormuz, signaling reduced odds of US ground troop deployment. Iran acknowledged that messages are being exchanged between both sides, with the country's president saying Iran is ready to end the war, though this contrasts with Tehran's public rhetoric. Iran continues demonstrating capacity to target critical Gulf infrastructure, evidenced by strikes against a Kuwaiti oil tanker.

COMPANY NEWS

MKC reported Q1 adjusted EPS of 66¢ vs Street 59¢ on revenue of $1.87B vs $1.79B, while formally announcing its combination with Unilever's food business. BIIB will acquire APLS for $41/share cash (~$5.6B) plus CVR worth up to $2/share. LLY will buy CNTA for $38/share cash (~$6.3B) plus CVR worth up to $9/share. CEG shares slumped after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations. FDS beat Q2 EPS expectations (4.46 vs 4.37) driven by 6.8% organic growth and raised full-year guidance. MRVL announced a deal with Nvidia for custom XPUs, with Nvidia investing $2B. SNX delivered massive Q1 EPS upside (4.73 vs 3.29) on sales of $17.16B vs $15.55B.
