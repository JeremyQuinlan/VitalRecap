Market Recap · Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 04:04 PM ET

MARKETS

Stocks posted substantial gains on Tuesday with the SPX climbing +3.85%, Nasdaq surging +4.42%, Dow advancing +2.49%, and R2K jumping +3.41%. Treasuries rallied alongside equities as yields dropped 3-7 basis points across the curve. DXY slumped -0.60%, Brent crude fell -2.70% to around $104.50, while gold climbed +3.85% and silver advanced +7.35%. Bitcoin ramped +1.75% to approximately $67.75K.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields declined 3-7 basis points across the curve as bonds gained alongside stocks. The market is now pricing in roughly 7 basis points of Fed Funds cuts this year, reflecting cautious optimism about deescalation prospects.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The rally was driven primarily by renewed hope for Middle East deescalation after Trump's social media confirmation of diplomatic progress with Iran (following Monday's WSJ report about exiting the conflict without militarily reopening Hormuz). This significantly reduces the odds of substantial ground troop deployment. While Iran continues public pushback, both sides acknowledge ongoing communications, and Tehran's president stated readiness to end the war. The combination of geopolitical relief and robust M&A activity ($90B announced this week alone) suggests corporate confidence remains intact despite macro volatility.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Trump effectively confirmed WSJ reporting about potential exit from Middle East conflict without militarily securing Hormuz, reducing ground deployment odds (WSJ)

• Iran acknowledged message exchanges with the US are occurring, with the country's president saying Iran is ready to end the war

• USS George HW Bush carrier group heading to Middle East to replace the USS Gerald Ford, which is undergoing repairs

• Iran demonstrated continued capacity to target Gulf infrastructure with strikes against a Kuwaiti oil tanker

COMPANY NEWS

BIIB announced acquisition of APLS for $41 per share cash at closing (~$5.6B) plus contingent value rights worth up to $2 per share, representing significant premium to Monday's $17.09 close.

LLY will acquire CNTA for $38 per share cash (~$6.3B) plus CVRs worth up to $9 per share (~$1.5B), totaling up to $47 versus Monday's $27.58 close.

MKC reported Q1 adjusted EPS of $0.66 versus Street estimate of $0.59, driven by revenue of $1.87B beating expectations of $1.79B, while formally announcing combination with Unilever's food business.

CEG shares underperformed after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations, with implied 2029 forecast also coming in light at analyst meeting.

FDS delivered Q2 EPS of $4.46 versus Street $4.37, driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth and robust 6.7% organic ASV growth, though operating margins fell 230 basis points year-over-year to 35%.
