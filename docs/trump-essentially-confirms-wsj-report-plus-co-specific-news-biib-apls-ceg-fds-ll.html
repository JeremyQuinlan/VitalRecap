COMPANY NEWS

MKC reported upside on Q1 adjusted EPS (66¢ vs. Street 59¢) driven by higher revenue ($1.87B vs. Street $1.79B), though organic growth of 1.2% fell short of the 1.4% expectation due to Flavor Solutions pressure. Gross margins expanded 100 basis points (20 basis points ahead of expectations) while operating margins rose 30 basis points. The lack of a guidance bump despite the beat might disappoint, though given negative staples sentiment, this is overall a victory. Multiple reports suggest a Unilever deal could be announced imminently.

NKE insists it doesn't plan to sell Converse, but Authentic Brands has expressed interest in buying the struggling business (Bloomberg). Investors would welcome a Converse divestiture given the brand's enormous struggles, though it won't bring in significant money with consensus modeling Converse revenue down 25% in F26 to $1.26B.

BIIB will acquire APLS for $41/share cash (~$5.6B) plus a CVR worth up to $2/share (APLS closed Monday at $17.09). The transaction is expected to strengthen Biogen's revenue and EPS growth potential, becoming increasingly accretive to non-GAAP EPS starting in 2027.

LLY will buy CNTA for $38/share cash (~$6.3B) plus a CVR worth up to $9/share for total value up to $47 (CNTA closed Monday at $27.58). Centessa is developing medicines for excessive daytime sleepiness and other neurological conditions.

CEG targets 20%+ base EPS CAGR from 2026-2029 and increased buyback authorization to $5B. For 2026, the company sees EPS of $11-12 with mid-point $11.50, slightly below consensus $11.72.

FDS reported modest Q2 EPS upside (4.46 vs. Street 4.37) driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth versus Street 6%, though operating margins fell short at 35% versus Street 35.5%. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $17.25-17.75 from prior $16.90-17.60.

SNX delivered strong Q1 EPS upside (4.73 vs. Street 3.29) with robust sales ($17.16B vs. Street $15.55B) and margin expansion. Q2 guidance mid-points are ahead with EPS of $4 versus Street $3.45.

T reiterated all 2026 and multi-year financial guidance plus capital return plans.

Swiss semiconductor supplier VAT Group issued mixed update with Q1 revenue tracking below guidance at CHF215MM versus prior CHF240-260MM forecast due to Middle East supply chain disruptions, though underlying demand remains strong with robust 1.6x book-to-bill.

Whoop was valued at $10.1B after a $575M capital raise, up from $3.6B valuation in 2021 (Bloomberg).
