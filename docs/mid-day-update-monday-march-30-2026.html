MARKETS

SPX advanced +0.55% while Nasdaq gained +0.31%. The Dow surged 412 points (+0.90%) and R2K declined 6 points (-0.25%). Equal-weight S&P outperformed at +0.76%. RATES & FED

MARKET OUTLOOK

US equities are fading from opening highs as investors remain skeptical of Trump's optimistic Iran rhetoric—every piece of Middle East news points to escalating conflict with boots on the ground likely in coming weeks. Until senior officials (JD, Marco, Steve, or Jared) board planes for Pakistan meetings with Iranian counterparts, Trump's "everything is swimmingly" talk will fall on deaf ears. Tech continues trading poorly for reasons mostly independent of geopolitics, while Germany's March CPI acceleration signals what's coming to global inflation gauges as war effects materialize. Powell's Harvard remarks reiterated his March 18th stance with a calmer tone—he still sees inflation ex-tariffs nearly at target while viewing tariffs and the oil shock as one-time events the Fed will look past.
