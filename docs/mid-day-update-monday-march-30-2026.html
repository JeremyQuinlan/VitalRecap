MARKETS

The SPX gained +0.55% while the Nasdaq advanced +0.31%. The Dow jumped +0.90% and the R2K slid -0.25%. Treasuries rallied with yields down 6-11 basis points across the curve. The DXY climbed 35 basis points, Brent rose ~60 basis points, gold advanced ~100 basis points and silver surged 170 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields fell 6-11 basis points across the curve in a sizeable rally. Fed fund futures still aren't pricing any rate moves this year, with expectations largely steady despite ongoing geopolitical tensions.

MARKET OUTLOOK

US equities are fading from opening highs as investors remain skeptical of Trump's optimistic Middle East pronouncements — until senior officials actually board planes for Pakistan talks with Iran, his "everything is swimmingly" rhetoric falls on deaf ears. The market assumes boots will hit the ground somewhere in the coming weeks, keeping risk sentiment fragile. Powell's calmer tone this morning (still seeing inflation ex-tariffs near target) suggests the Fed will look past oil shocks and tariffs as one-time events. Tech continues struggling for reasons mostly independent of geopolitics, while Germany's sharp March CPI acceleration signals what's coming globally as war effects hit data.
