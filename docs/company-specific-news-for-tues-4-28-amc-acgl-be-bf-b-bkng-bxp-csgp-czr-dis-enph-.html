MARKET OUTLOOK

The earnings landscape painted a decidedly bullish picture Tuesday night, with robust results from major consumer names like SBUX, MDLZ, and V showcasing remarkable consumer resilience despite macro headwinds. The memory trade is reigniting after STX's blowout results, while semiconductor strength continues with NXPI clearing high expectations. Consumer spending data from Visa was particularly encouraging — management sees "both discretionary and non-discretionary spend remain strong" with no signs of weakening among lower-spend consumers. This earnings momentum, combined with broad-based strength across sectors, suggests the market's underlying fundamentals remain solid heading into the peak earnings stretch.

COMPANY NEWS

BKNG reported modest EBITDA upside but bookings fell short at $53.8B vs. Street $54.2B due to Middle East pressure clipping ~200 basis points off growth. Full-year guidance tweaked lower on Iran-related disruptions and FX headwinds.

STX delivered blowout Q3 EPS of $4.10 vs. Street $3.50 on robust sales of $3.11B vs. $2.95B expected, with gross margins hitting 47% — more than 200 basis points above consensus. Q4 guidance bullish at $5 EPS vs. Street $3.96.

V posted solid Q2 EPS upside at $3.31 vs. Street $3.10, driven by robust revenue of $11.2B vs. $10.7B expected. Full-year EPS guide increased to low-teens growth vs. prior low-double-digits. Revenue growth of 17% was highest since 2022.

SBUX reported strong Q2 EPS of 50¢ vs. Street 43¢, with blowout US comps of +7.1% vs. Street +3.7% (transactions +4.3%, pricing +2.7%). Operating margins climbed 120 basis points to 9.4% vs. Street 8.2%. China comps disappointed at +0.5% vs. +3.4% expected.

MDLZ delivered solid EPS upside at 67¢ vs. Street 61¢, with organic growth of 3% vs. Street +0.35%. All geographies outperformed on "strong" emerging market performance and "signs of improvement" in developed markets.

NXPI faced high bar post-TXN strength but exceeded expectations with Q1 EPS of $3.05 vs. Street $2.99. More importantly, Q2 guidance robust at $3.50 EPS vs. Street $3.22 with sales of $3.45B vs. $3.27B expected.

HOOD reported Q1 EPS shortfall at 38¢ vs. Street 42¢ and revenue of $1.07B vs. $1.141B expected. Crypto revenue fell 47% while options grew only 8%, though event contract revenue surged 320% to $147MM. Full-year expense guide up $100MM on Trump Account-related work.

DIS decided against spinning out ESPN (Business Insider).
