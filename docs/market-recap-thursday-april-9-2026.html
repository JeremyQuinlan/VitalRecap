Market Recap · Thursday April 9, 2026

Apr 09, 2026 · 04:05 PM ET

MARKETS

The SPX climbed +0.62% while the equal-weight version managed only +0.08%, highlighting the narrow nature of Thursday's advance. The Nasdaq jumped +0.83%, the Dow gained +0.58%, and R2K rose +0.61%. Brent oil surged ~2.60% to ~$97.20 (though off session highs near $99.50), gold advanced +1.10%, silver jumped +2.10%, and Bitcoin climbed +1.00% to ~$72K. The DXY dropped -0.37%, extending recent losses.

RATES & FED

Treasuries were relatively quiet with yields flat to up/down 1 basis point across the curve. Fed expectations held fairly steady despite the day's geopolitical developments and mixed economic data.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The SPX has already bounced ~8% trough-to-peak in just a few days and is approaching the problematic 6900-7000 zone that was resistance even before the war started. While we're more optimistic than consensus on Middle East ceasefire durability (which should pressure markets higher given lean positioning and negative sentiment), the region will never return to pre-war conditions—$90 is probably the new $70 for Brent. A substantial burst of stagflationary pressure is washing over the global economy from five weeks of fighting, with Friday's March CPI expected to show significant acceleration. The technical backdrop remains supportive, but we're getting into challenging territory.

GEOPOLITICAL

Netanyahu said Israel would seek "direct negotiations" with Lebanon, with reports suggesting Trump is urging the Israeli government to ease back on the Hezbollah campaign (NBC News). The Iran ceasefire appears to be holding with no reports of strikes on Iran and no Iranian drone/missile launches, while Iranian and Saudi foreign ministers spoke Thursday morning for the first time since the war commenced. Trump told NBC News he was "very optimistic" about reaching a deal with Iran, saying Iranian leaders are "much more reasonable" in meetings than to the press. Saturday's Pakistan meeting between U.S. officials (led by Vance) and Tehran is proceeding as planned.

COMPANY NEWS

BB reported upside on Q4 revenue/EBITDA of $156MM/$36.1MM versus Street estimates of $144.1MM/$28.8MM, with fiscal 2027 guidance mid-points slightly ahead including EBITDA of $120MM versus Street $116.5MM.

BF.B surged on M&A speculation after the WSJ reported the company was approached about a potential deal by privately held Sazerac (WSJ).

AMZN CEO Andy Jassy published his annual letter with an extremely bullish tone on AI demand, especially the firm's opportunity in custom silicon.

STZ rose on earnings while SMPL fell on disappointing results. Legacy software stocks were crushed amid anxiety over Anthropic's Project Glasswing initiative, which the company says is so powerful it can't be released without preparing the industry for its implications (Axios reports OpenAI will adopt a similar approach).
