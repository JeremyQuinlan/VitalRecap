MARKET OUTLOOK

The industrials sector is painting a remarkably healthy picture, with solid-to-strong results and guidance across EME, GD, GNRC, IEX, LII, SLGN, SWK, and VMC. The chip and component optimism from APH and AVT builds on yesterday's strength from NXPI and STX, suggesting the semiconductor recovery has real legs. While GEHC's margin miss and lowered guidance due to memory chip, oil, and freight cost pressures serves as a reminder that inflationary headwinds remain, the breadth of industrial strength is encouraging for the broader economic narrative.

COMPANY NEWS

ABBV reported modest Q1 EPS upside at $2.65 versus Street $2.60, with sales of $15B beating the $14.7B estimate on strength in Immunology, Neuroscience, and Aesthetics. Full-year EPS guidance raised to $14.08-14.28 from prior $13.96-14.16.

ADP delivered solid Q3 EPS upside at $3.37 versus $3.31 expected, driven by higher sales ($5.939B vs $5.851B) and 80 basis point margin expansion to 30.2%. FY guidance increased to EPS growth of 10-11% from prior 9-10%.

APH posted strong Q1 results with EPS of $1.06 versus Street $0.95, powered by 33% organic sales growth to $7.6B against $7.11B expected. Q2 guidance midpoints ahead of plan at $1.15 EPS and $8.15B revenue.

BIIB crushed Q1 expectations with EPS of $3.57 versus $2.93 expected and sales of $2.47B versus $2.249B, thanks to impressive growth products performance including Leqembi up 74%, Zurzuvae up 100%, and Qalsody up 110%.

GD reported strong Q1 upside with EPS of $4.10 versus Street $3.69, driven by 10.3% revenue growth to $13.48B and backlog surging 47.6% year-over-year to ~$130.8B with robust 2x book-to-bill.

GEHC disappointed with Q1 EPS miss at 99¢ versus Street $1.06 as EBIT margins fell 130 basis points below consensus. Full-year EPS guide cut due to "significant increases in memory chips, oil and freight costs during the first quarter."

GNRC delivered strong EBITDA upside at $193MM versus Street $161MM, with 240 basis point margin expansion and robust C&I growth of 28% to $510MM. Management noting momentum in data center markets with hyperscale customer approvals progressing.

LULU faces renewed governance criticism from founder Chip Wilson, who attacked the board over CEO selection, calling it "broken governance" and criticizing the hiring of Heidi O'Neill as doubling down on "broken strategy."

YUM beat Q1 expectations with 3% comps versus Street 2.65%, powered by exceptional Taco Bell performance at 8% comps versus 5.1% expected, while EPS of $1.50 exceeded the $1.39 estimate.

WING reported mixed results with Q1 EPS upside at $1.18 versus $1.03 expected, but domestic comps fell 8.7% versus Street expectation of -5.4%. Full-year comp guidance cut to down 1-3% from prior flat-to-up 3%.
