COMPANY NEWS

NKE delivered a solid Q3 beat with EPS of 35 cents versus Street expectations of 30 cents, driven primarily by margin outperformance rather than sales strength. While gross margins declined 130 basis points to 40.2%, this wasn't as severe as the Street's 39.8% forecast, and sales fell roughly in line with expectations at -3% on a foreign exchange neutral basis versus consensus of -2.6%. Geographically, North America outperformed (+3% vs. Street +2.75%) and China wasn't as disastrous as feared (-10% vs. Street -15.75%), though EMEA disappointed significantly at -7% versus Street expectations of -1.3%. Footwear performed better than expected while apparel lagged, and Converse continues to be a major drag with revenue plummeting 37%.

PLAY stumbled badly in Q4 with overall sales dropping 0.9% to $530 million (versus Street $560 million), comps declining 3.3% (versus Street -1.2%), and EBITDA coming in at $111.4 million versus consensus of $126 million. Management blamed winter storms for the underperformance, claiming that without weather headwinds comps would have been -1.5% with EBITDA of $116 million (still missing consensus but not as dramatically). Despite the weak results, management struck a confident tone about their "back-to-basics strategy" and guided for improved comps, revenue, and EBITDA in fiscal 2026, projecting over $100 million in free cash flow—far above Street consensus of $20 million and representing roughly 27% of current market cap.

PVH crushed Q4 expectations with EPS of $3.82 versus Street $3.31, powered by strong sales growth (+6% to $2.505 billion vs. Street $2.435 billion) and margin expansion (operating margins roughly 100 basis points ahead of consensus at 10%). The company's 2026 guidance also came in slightly above expectations with EPS of $11.95 versus Street $11.86, operating margins of 8.8% versus Street 8.6%, and sales projected to be "up slightly" compared to consensus +1%.

RH disappointed across the board in Q4 with sales of $843 million falling short of Street expectations of $873.6 million and EBITDA margins of 17.7% missing consensus of 18.5%. The fiscal 2027 guidance was particularly concerning, with management projecting sales growth of +6% at the midpoint versus Street expectations of +10.1% and EBITDA margins of 15% versus Street 17.5%.
