MARKET OUTLOOK

Ending the Iran conflict will be positive for equities, especially given recent technical developments with oversold prices and sentiment swinging aggressively negative. However, the SPX was already facing stiff resistance around 6900-7000 even before Iran escalated (thanks to private credit concerns, AI doubts, and the massive IPO backlog), so markets need to be kept on a short lease. Getting back to 6900 represents the best-case scenario right now — only about a 6% rally from current levels.

GEOPOLITICAL

Iran continues waging economic terror throughout the Gulf, but Trump appears positioned to extract the US from the conflict as poll numbers deteriorate and Congressional resistance builds against a massive $200 billion supplemental package. The WSJ reported the US is walking away without militarily opening the Strait of Hormuz, which Trump effectively confirmed via social media and remarks to reporters — a positive development since there's no easy military solution for securing the waterway. Iran's reparation demands could be met through countries paying its Hormuz toll requirements (just a few dollars per barrel), while security guarantees can be granted assuming they're conditioned on Tehran never reconstituting missile and nuclear programs. The Pentagon is reportedly running out of targets to strike within days, potentially allowing them to declare victory, though Israel signals plans to occupy southern Lebanon once fighting ends.
