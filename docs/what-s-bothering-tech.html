MARKET OUTLOOK

Tech stocks are facing a perfect storm of headwinds that extends well beyond Middle East geopolitics. The sector's underperformance reflects a fundamental shift in investor sentiment, driven by supply chain disruptions, architectural changes in data centers, and growing skepticism about AI economics. OpenAI's strategic pivot toward a leaner model could signal broader industry maturation, potentially dampening the infrastructure build-out that has driven much of the recent growth. The combination of regulatory pushback, massive IPO supply coming to market, and persistent questions about AI's return on investment suggests tech's dominance may be entering a more challenging phase.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Iran and Middle East tensions are creating the primary market narrative, with Gulf countries potentially scaling back AI investments and data center construction due to regional conflicts

• War-related supply chain disruptions are severely impacting helium supplies, a critical component for high-end chip manufacturing

COMPANY NEWS

Meta and YouTube faced a significant legal setback this week when a jury found both companies liable for releasing intentionally addictive products, raising concerns about a potential "Tobacco Moment" for social media platforms that could trigger widespread litigation across the industry.
