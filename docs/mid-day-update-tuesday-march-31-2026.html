MARKETS

The SPX climbed +1.15% while the Nasdaq surged +1.55%, though equal-weight SPX lagged with just +0.57% gains. The Dow advanced 345 points (+0.77%) and R2K jumped 33 points (+1.37%). Brent crude rose to ~$107.75 (+0.30%), gold advanced ~2.00%, silver climbed 4.50%, and Bitcoin traded flat. The DXY dipped 40 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields were flat to down 4 basis points across the curve. The market is pricing in 7 basis points of Fed rate cuts for the year, reflecting cautious optimism about the geopolitical situation potentially deescalating.

MARKET OUTLOOK

This rally has legs if Trump really does walk away from Iran without securing Hormuz — markets feared a sustained ground campaign more than anything else. Yes, it leaves a critical waterway in hostile hands, but it dramatically reduces the risk of Iran escalating attacks on regional energy infrastructure. The rotation into risk assets (chips, internet, banks, travel) while utilities and defensive names lag tells the story. Nothing's guaranteed with Iran, but the market is betting on de-escalation within the administration's 4-6 week timeline.
