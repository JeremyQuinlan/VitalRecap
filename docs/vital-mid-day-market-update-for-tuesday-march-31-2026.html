Vital Mid-Day Market Update for Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 11:04 AM ET

MARKETS

The SPX gained +1.15% with the Nasdaq up +1.55%, though equal-weight SPX lagged at just +0.57%. The Dow advanced 345 points (+0.77%) while the R2K climbed +1.37%. Brent oil rose to ~$107.75 (+0.30%), gold surged +2.00%, silver jumped +4.50%, while Bitcoin stayed flat. The DXY dipped -0.40%.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields moved flat to down 4 basis points as markets priced in 7 basis points of Fed rate cuts for the year. The move reflected some relief over potential de-escalation in the Middle East conflict.

MARKET OUTLOOK

The rally is primarily driven by Trump administration signals it could wind down the Iran war without securing the Strait of Hormuz — leaving the waterway with Tehran but reducing odds of a sustained ground campaign that markets feared most (WSJ). This doesn't guarantee anything and the situation remains extremely fluid, but it suggests lower probability of the kind of escalation that would devastate regional economic infrastructure. Away from geopolitics, the day's other positives included better-than-feared Eurozone CPI and cooling US home prices that signal housing remains an important domestic disinflation source.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Trump tells aides he's willing to walk away from Iran war without reopening Hormuz, as seizing control would require extended military campaign beyond the administration's 4-6 week timeline (WSJ)

• Iran's remaining government struggling to make decisions following ongoing war and assassination of senior leaders, though security services continue functioning while hardline IRGC elements assert greater control (NYT)

• Gulf allies pressing White House to continue fighting until Iran is decisively defeated (AP)

• Iran pushing Houthis to resume Red Sea shipping campaign should US escalate further (Bloomberg)

COMPANY NEWS

BIIB announced acquisition of APLS (Apellis) for $41/share cash (~$5.6B) plus CVR worth up to $2/share, with APLS closing Monday at $17.09. LLY will buy CNTA (Centessa) for $38/share cash (~$6.3B) plus CVR up to $9/share for total value up to $47, with CNTA closing Monday at $27.58.

MKC (McCormick) reported Q1 adjusted EPS of 66¢ vs Street 59¢ on revenue of $1.87B vs Street $1.79B, reiterating full-year guidance. The company formally announced plans to combine with Unilever's food business in a Reverse Morris Trust structure with Unilever owning ~2/3 of the new entity (WSJ).

FDS (FactSet) delivered Q2 EPS of $4.46 vs Street $4.37 driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth vs Street 6%, though operating margins fell 230 basis points year-over-year to 35% vs Street 35.5%. CEG (Constellation Energy) underperformed after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations.

MRVL (Marvell) announced deal with Nvidia to provide custom XPUs and NVLink Fusion-compatible networking, with Nvidia investing $2B into Marvell. SNAP faces activist pressure as Irenic builds a stake pushing for changes (Bloomberg).
