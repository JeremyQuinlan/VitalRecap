Vital Mid-Day Market Update for Tuesday March 31, 2026

Mar 31, 2026 · 11:04 AM ET

MARKETS

SPX up +1.15%, Nasdaq +1.55%, Dow +345 points/+0.77%, R2K +33 points/+1.37%. Brent crude climbed ~30 basis points to $107.75, gold advanced ~2.00%, silver surged 4.50%. Bitcoin remained roughly flat while DXY dipped 40 basis points.

RATES & FED

Treasury yields held flat to down 4 basis points as markets priced in 7 basis points of Fed rate cuts for the year. The move reflects cautious optimism around geopolitical developments potentially reducing pressure on the central bank to maintain an aggressive stance.

MARKET OUTLOOK

Stocks are seeing decent gains largely driven by WSJ reporting that Trump could wind down the Iran war without securing Hormuz, suggesting lower odds of a sustained ground campaign (which had been a major market fear). While nothing is guaranteed and the Iran situation remains extremely fluid, this signals reduced risk of Iranian retaliation against critical regional economic infrastructure. The equal-weight SPX lagging at just 57 basis points shows this is still a narrow rally, but the breadth across sectors suggests cautious relief is taking hold.

GEOPOLITICAL

• Trump tells aides he's willing to walk away from the Iran war without reopening Hormuz, as seizing control would require an extended military campaign beyond the administration's 4-6 week timeline (WSJ)

• Iran's remaining government struggles to make decisions following ongoing war and assassination of senior leaders, though security services continue functioning while hardline IRGC elements assert greater control (NYT)

• Iran pushing Houthis to resume Red Sea shipping attacks should the US escalate further (Bloomberg)

• Gulf allies pressing White House to continue fighting until Iran is decisively defeated (AP)

COMPANY NEWS

MKC reported Q1 adjusted EPS upside at 66¢ versus Street's 59¢, driven by revenue of $1.87B versus $1.79B expected, while formally announcing plans to combine with Unilever's food business in a Reverse Morris Trust structure where Unilever will own roughly two-thirds of the new entity (WSJ). BIIB will acquire APLS for $41/share cash (~$5.6B) plus CVR worth up to $2/share, a significant premium to Monday's $17.09 close. LLY buying CNTA for $38/share cash (~$6.3B) plus CVR worth up to $9/share for total potential value of $47 versus Monday's $27.58 close. FDS delivered modest Q2 EPS upside at $4.46 versus Street's $4.37, with the beat driven by 6.8% organic revenue growth versus 6% expected and robust organic ASV growth of 6.7% versus 5.8% expected, though operating margins fell slightly short. MRVL announced a deal with Nvidia for custom XPUs and NVLink Fusion-compatible networking, with Nvidia investing $2B into Marvell. CEG shares underperformed after providing 2026 EPS guidance slightly below expectations.
