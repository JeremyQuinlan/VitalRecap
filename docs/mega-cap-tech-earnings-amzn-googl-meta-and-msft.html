MARKET OUTLOOK

The mega-cap tech earnings present a mixed but generally constructive picture for the broader market. While none of these names delivered knockout quarters, the consistency of operating leverage (particularly at GOOGL and META) demonstrates these companies can still expand margins even as growth moderates. The real test will be whether cloud acceleration at Google and AWS can offset concerns about elevated capex spending across the cohort.

COMPANY NEWS

AMZN delivered solid Q1 results with operating income of $23.9B versus Street expectations of $20.7B, while revenue grew 17% to $181.5B (vs $177.2B expected). The standout was AWS, which accelerated 400 basis points sequentially to 28% growth with margins strengthening to 37.7%. However, Q2 operating income guidance of $20-24B came in slightly below the $22.85B consensus, creating a modest headwind.

GOOGL posted impressive operating leverage with margins expanding 200 basis points to 36.1%, driving operating income to $39.7B versus $36.1B expected. The real story was Cloud revenue surging 63% to $20B (well ahead of $18.4B estimates) with backlog nearly doubling quarter-over-quarter to over $460B. Search remained solid at $60.4B revenue, though YouTube Ads disappointed.

META showed strong execution with operating income of $22.8B beating the $19.5B estimate, driven by 33% revenue growth to $56.3B and robust free cash flow of $12.4B. The company maintained 41% operating margins despite heavy AI investments. However, user counts declined quarter-over-quarter (attributed to internet disruptions in Iran and WhatsApp restrictions in Russia), and 2026 capex guidance increased to $125-144B from the prior $115-135B range.

MSFT delivered steady results with operating income of $38.4B versus $37B expected, supported by 18% revenue growth to $82.9B. Azure growth of 39% slightly exceeded expectations, and total AI revenue reached $37B in ARR (up 123%). The concerning element was commercial bookings declining 6%, a sharp deceleration from recent quarters that suggests some demand softening ahead.
