COMPANY NEWS

DOLE delivered inline Q1 EPS and EBITDA with full-year EBITDA guidance unchanged at $400MM. Management acknowledges a more "complex" operating environment with Q2 cost pressures but cites "robust consumer demand" in key markets.

PRKS missed Q1 EBITDA at $58MM versus Street expectations of $62.5MM, hurt by unfavorable weather and declining international attendance. Management remained aggressive on buybacks, calling the stock "materially undervalued," while expressing confidence in forward indicators despite Q1 headwinds.

B posted strong Q1 adjusted EPS upside at 98¢ versus Street estimate of 80¢, driven by higher gold production (719K ounces versus guidance of 640-680K) and lower per-ounce costs. The company announced a new $3B buyback while keeping full-year outlook unchanged.

CEG beat with adjusted EPS of $2.74 versus Street estimate of $2.53, while reiterating full-year EPS guidance of $11-12.

MOS reported inline Q1 EBITDA of $417MM, down roughly 24% year-over-year. The company withdrew 2026 phosphate production guidance and initiated partial production curtailments at Louisiana and Bartow facilities while scaling back Brazilian operations.

BZH received a $25.75 per share cash takeout bid from DFH worth approximately $704MM. Dream Finders CEO Patrick Zalupski said the combination would create the seventh-largest U.S. homebuilder with "highly complementary footprints."

CRCL beat Q1 EBITDA at $151MM versus Street estimate of $138MM but missed on revenue growth of 20% to $694MM (versus $721MM expected), representing sharp deceleration from Q4. The company announced a $222MM ARC token presale at $3B valuation.

FSK missed adjusted net income per share at 41¢ versus 44¢ expected, with NAV falling to $18.83 from $20.89 at year-end. Non-accrual investments rose to 4.2% from 3.4%, while dividend dropped to 42¢ from 48¢. The company announced a $300MM buyback alongside KKR's agreement to waive 100% of subordinated income incentive fees, plus KKR will tender $150MM at $11 per share.

MNDY surged in premarket on solid beats across revenue (+24% to $351.3MM versus $339MM expected), margins (14% operating margin versus 11.2% expected), and raised full-year guidance to 19-20% revenue growth from prior 18-19%. Management highlighted AI productivity gains enabling revenue growth without proportional headcount increases.

SATS beat Q1 adjusted OIBDA at $493MM versus $473MM expected but posted weak subscriber metrics, losing 366K TV subs while adding only 16K wireless subs. The stock story centers on its 2.8-3% SpaceX stake acquired through spectrum sales rather than core operations.
