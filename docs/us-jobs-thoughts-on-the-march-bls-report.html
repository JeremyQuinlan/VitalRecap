MARKET OUTLOOK

The March employment report presents a classic bulls versus bears narrative. While the 178K job gain demonstrates impressive economic resilience despite elevated macro uncertainty, it doesn't cross the threshold that would force investors to seriously consider rate hikes. That said, the underlying details reveal concerning weakness in the Household Survey (64K drop in employed people, 396K decline in labor force participation). The real risk lies in whether this labor strength, combined with Iran war-related inflation pressures, forces the Fed to consider tightening rather than easing.
